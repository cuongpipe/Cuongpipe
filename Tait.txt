Two major technology trends that will greatly impact our future are Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Cloud Computing.

AI helps machines learn and make decisions like humans. It's used in tools like ChatGPT, Google Translate, and facial recognition apps. In the next 5–10 years, AI may help programmers write code or detect bugs. In 20 years, it might build complex systems almost on its own.

Cloud Computing allows users to store and run apps online. I use Google Drive and GitHub, which run on the cloud. In the future, most software may be cloud-based, and we may no longer need to install apps.

These trends will shape my job as a software engineer. AI will help me work faster, while Cloud Computing will change how I build and deploy software.

To adapt, I plan to learn Python and AI tools like TensorFlow, and practice small AI projects. I’ll also study cloud platforms like AWS, Docker, and Kubernetes, and deploy my personal projects online.

I recently gave advice to two friends buying laptops for different needs.

One has a 20 million VND budget for gaming. I recommended the MSI GF63, with Core i7, 16GB RAM, and GTX 1650. It runs games smoothly but has short battery life (3–5 hours).

The other has a 10 million VND budget for office work. I suggested the Acer Aspire 5, with Ryzen 5, 8GB RAM, and SSD. It runs office tasks well and has good battery life (6–8 hours).
